Blood Groups and Compatibility

Introduction to Blood Groups

Human blood is divided into different groups based on the presence of antigens on red blood cells. The ABO blood group system includes four major types: A, B, AB, and O. Each blood type can also be positive (+) or negative (-), depending on the Rh factor.

Understanding blood group compatibility is extremely important during blood transfusions because receiving incompatible blood can cause serious medical complications.

Universal Donor

People with blood type O negative (O-) are called universal donors because their blood can be given to patients of any blood group. This makes O- blood extremely important in emergency situations.

Universal Recipient

People with blood type AB positive (AB+) are known as universal recipients because they can receive blood from all blood groups. This makes transfusions easier for patients with AB+ blood type.

Importance of Blood Donation

Knowing your blood group helps doctors provide safe transfusions during emergencies. Regular blood donation helps hospitals maintain an adequate supply of blood for patients who require life-saving treatment.
