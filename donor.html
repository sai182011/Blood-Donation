Why Become a Blood Donor?

Becoming a blood donor is one of the most meaningful ways to help others. Every day hospitals require blood for surgeries, accident victims, and patients suffering from life-threatening diseases.

By registering as a blood donor, you can help save lives and support the healthcare system. A single blood donation can save up to three lives because the donated blood can be separated into red blood cells, plasma, and platelets.

Who Can Donate Blood?

Most healthy adults between the ages of 18 and 65 can donate blood. The donor should weigh at least 50 kg and must not be suffering from serious infections or illnesses. Donors should also eat a healthy meal and drink plenty of water before donating blood.
