Blood Donation Eligibility

Who Can Donate Blood?

Blood donation is a safe process, but donors must meet certain eligibility requirements to ensure both the donor and recipient remain healthy. The following guidelines help determine whether a person is eligible to donate blood.

Basic Eligibility Requirements

  • Age must be between 18 and 65 years.
  • The donor must weigh at least 50 kilograms.
  • The donor should be in good general health.
  • Hemoglobin level should be within a safe range.
  • The donor should not have serious infections or diseases.

Who Should Not Donate Blood?

  • People suffering from serious illnesses.
  • Individuals with certain infections such as hepatitis.
  • People who recently underwent major surgery.
  • Pregnant women or women who recently gave birth.
  • Individuals who recently donated blood (less than 3 months).

Before Donating Blood

Before donating blood, it is important to eat a healthy meal and drink plenty of water. Donors should avoid alcohol and heavy exercise before donation. A good night's sleep is also recommended to ensure the donor feels comfortable.

After Donating Blood

After the donation process, donors should rest for a few minutes and drink fluids such as juice or water. Heavy physical activity should be avoided for the rest of the day. Following these simple precautions helps ensure a safe and positive blood donation experience.

Why Eligibility Matters

Eligibility rules help protect both donors and recipients. Blood collected from healthy donors ensures that the transfusion process remains safe and effective for patients who require medical treatment.
